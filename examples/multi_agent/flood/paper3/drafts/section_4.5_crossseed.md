**Patterns were stable across the three random seeds (42, 123, and 456), indicating that the main results do not depend on a single initialization.** Across the pooled three-seed experiment, the LLM-ABM reproduced the same qualitative contrasts reported above: convergence on rare or tightly constrained actions, limited aggregate sensitivity to institutional endogenization, and theory-consistent construct-behavior associations.

**RQ1 was robust because the convergence-divergence classification was unchanged under seed variation.** The three convergence cases, owner elevation, owner buyout, and renter contents insurance, showed limited cross-seed dispersion, with annual standard deviations of 0.003-0.013, 0.003-0.008, and 0.003-0.095. The divergence cases also remained distinct from the traditional ABM across seeds: owner insurance ranged from 0.006 to 0.058, renter relocation from 0.003 to 0.035, and renter do-nothing from 0.012 to 0.100. These ranges remain small relative to the pooled mean absolute differences for owner insurance (15.6 pp), renter relocation (20.8 pp), and renter do-nothing (20.2 pp).

**RQ2 was robust because the affordability-blocking pattern persisted across the three-seed experiment.** Marginalized owners remained the group most likely to propose actions that could not be executed after affordability checks. In the pooled three-seed summaries, affordability blocking for marginalized households ranged from 2.56% under the Full condition to 6.33% under the Flat condition, compared with 0.00%-0.49% for non-marginalized households. This is consistent with institutional endogenization mattering more through the equity channel than through aggregate adoption rates.

**RQ3 was also robust because the same construct-behavior relationships held in the pooled three-seed data.** Stakeholder perception remained positively associated with protective behavior, with mean SP of 2.78 for insurance decisions versus 2.45 for do-nothing, and SP retained a strong flood-dose relationship (rho = 0.458) despite a weak temporal trend (rho = 0.097). Place attachment also retained the expected association with renter relocation, with mean PA of 2.61 for relocation versus 2.76 for renter do-nothing. These results are consistent with SP and PA functioning as the clearest unconstrained constructs.

**The robustness analysis should still be interpreted as preliminary because three seeds are a minimum sensitivity check, not an exhaustive stochastic test.** Future work should expand this analysis to at least 10 seeds and report seed-level intervals for subgroup contrasts and construct-behavior associations.
