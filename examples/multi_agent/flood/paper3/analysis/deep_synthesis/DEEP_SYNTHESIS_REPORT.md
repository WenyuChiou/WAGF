# Deep Synthesis Report

## 1. Data integrity

This synthesis covers six completed arms and uses executed outcomes only. Five arms contain the expected 2,600 owner decisions and 2,600 renter decisions. The only discrepancy is `CLN_Flat_123`, which contains 2,599 owner traces and 2,600 renter traces, so the cross-arm synthesis rests on 31,199 household decisions rather than the nominal 31,200. The three Full arms also include 13 government and 13 insurance traces each, while the three Flat arms are household-only by design and encode fixed institutional conditions through household `environment_context`. All six `reproducibility_manifest.json` files report `git_dirty=false`. The run-level commits represented in the manifests are `14b55a6f5ea797cce600b3cf6af610a73b4c929a`, `5609508e87a0d9114df07821e5c0b7b9be676ff4`, `d4a6a78158b4fe90b570ee1455217f5bd9722363`, and `ebce57da91480d6fd38d9a9580fef620de89c0d0`; the repository head used for synthesis is `9fd89ba`. Phase A closed the only missing analysis surface by fully populating `gemma4_clean_seed123`. Every headline value below comes from `approved_skill.skill_name`, `final` state variables such as `has_insurance`, `elevated`, and `relocated`, or survey `pa_score` for PA analyses. Proposed skills are not used for any headline number, which keeps the synthesis aligned with the governance architecture claim of Paper 3.

## 2. Executive summary

Three results dominate the 6-arm panel. First, ratchet language is primarily a memory-policy phenomenon. At Year 13, `LEG_Full_42` shows past-reference rates of `0.190` for owners and `0.455` for renters, while CLEAN Full averages `0.018` and `0.086`, and CLEAN Flat remains similarly compressed at `0.018` and `0.085`. Second, institutional flattening changes household response levels inside CLEAN but does not create a new behavioral regime. For MG-Owner households, pooled CLEAN Full Y13 insurance is `0.290`, pooled CLEAN Flat is `0.257`, and LEGACY Full is higher at `0.390`. The matching do-nothing rates are `0.675`, `0.703`, and `0.602`. Third, calibration should be treated descriptively rather than as a pass-fail gate. LEGACY Full EPI is `0.6115`, CLEAN Full mean EPI is `0.5542`, and CLEAN Flat mean EPI is `0.2678`. Flat policy moves `insurance_rate_sfha` from `0.829` toward `0.787`, closer to the empirical ceiling of `0.60`, but both remain high. The balanced reading is therefore dual-role: permissive memory sustains self-referential continuity and persistence, while restrictive memory suppresses ratchet phrases but also compresses the action mix.

## 3. RQ1 cross-paradigm

The dual-timeseries figure should frame the cross-paradigm result as a pattern comparison rather than a single benchmark contest. Relative to Traditional FLOODABM, all LLM arms remain more insurance-heavy and far less structurally adaptive, especially on elevation and buyout. Within the LLM family, memory policy creates the sharpest contrast on long-horizon narrative persistence, while institutional policy mainly shifts levels. `LEG_Full_42` is the stickiest arm because permissive memory lets previous choices and self-descriptions echo into later reasoning and later executed action. CLEAN Full strips the risky write types but keeps endogenous subsidy and CRS dynamics, showing how much insurance persistence survives without the ratchet. CLEAN Flat then fixes subsidy at `0.50` and CRS discount at `0.0`, exposing a smaller but still visible institutional effect: slightly less insurance persistence and slightly more renter relocation. This layered comparison is stronger than the earlier two-arm view because it lets Paper 3 compare memory, institution, and traditional baseline in one panel. The right headline is not that one model wins, but that different policy and memory settings occupy different parts of the same behavioral space.

## 4. RQ2 institutional effect

The institutional comparison is identified only within CLEAN, and it should be reported as a level shift with uncertainty rather than as a regime break. On the key over-insurance benchmark, `insurance_rate_sfha` averages `0.829` in CLEAN Full and `0.787` in CLEAN Flat, moving toward the empirical ceiling when endogenous institutions are removed. For the MG-Owner cell, do-nothing averages `0.675` in CLEAN Full and `0.703` in CLEAN Flat, while Y13 insurance moves from `0.290` to `0.257`. Those differences are coherent but modest relative to the LEGACY-to-CLEAN memory shift. The pooled Mann-Whitney tests reinforce that interpretation. For MG-Owner do-nothing, `U=4,930,900`, `p=0.0189`, and rank-biserial correlation is `-0.027`; for MG-Owner insurance, `U=5,207,150`, `p=0.0206`, and rank-biserial correlation is `0.027`. These are statistically detectable because the decision counts are large, but the effect sizes are small. The manuscript should therefore present endogenous subsidy and CRS dynamics as tuning household response inside CLEAN, not as the dominant source of behavioral mode. Institutions matter, but memory policy sets the larger envelope within which that institutional effect operates.

## 5. RQ3 construct-action mechanism

RQ3 is anchored on survey `pa_score`, not LLM PA labels. That matters because the mechanism claim should not depend on a narrative variable that the memory policy itself can reshape. TP, CP, SP, and SC remain LLM-ordinal, but PA is treated as a stable survey trait. Within that setup, SP remains coupled to executed protection across all six arms. Cramer's V stays in a narrow band: `0.405` for `LEG_Full_42`, `0.415` and `0.404` for CLEAN Full, and `0.416`, `0.409`, and `0.384` for CLEAN Flat. Protection rates rise from roughly `0.36-0.39` in the SP low/medium group to `0.81-0.85` in the SP high/very-high group in every arm. The within-agent transition table shows the same mechanism dynamically. When SP moves up, switch-to-protective rates increase from `0.042-0.055` to `0.061-0.099`, with chi-squared values from `5.69` to `29.97` depending on arm. The key result for Section 4 is therefore not that memory changes whether stakeholder perception matters, but that memory changes how strongly those construct shifts persist once a protective action pattern has begun.

## 6. Dual-role evidence

The dual-role interpretation should be presented side by side, not as a verdict for one policy. On the rationalization side, `LEG_Full_42` retains much more past-reference language than either CLEAN condition. At Year 13, owner and renter past-reference rates are `0.190` and `0.455` in LEGACY, versus CLEAN Full means of `0.018` and `0.086` and CLEAN Flat means of `0.018` and `0.085`. On the memory-content side, LEGACY accumulates all nine content types, while CLEAN suppresses `agent_self_report`, `agent_reflection_quote`, and `initial_narrative` to zero or structural zero. That is the ratchet-control story. But the same write policy also affects behavioral diversity. Under CLEAN, buyout remains essentially absent, elevation stays rare, and renter relocation only partially expands under Flat. The consequence is that blocked write types are not just stylistic fluff. They also function as continuity carriers that support more differentiated long-horizon trajectories. Paper 3 should therefore say both things explicitly: permissive memory increases self-referential persistence and ratchet language, while restrictive memory reduces that language but also narrows the available action repertoire. The data support both roles, and the synthesis should not frame one policy as simply correct.

## 7. Cross-cutting findings

The cross-cutting file resolves six questions that matter for how Section 4 should be written. First, the institutional effect is not testable under LEGACY because there is no LEGACY Flat arm; that is a design limitation, not a missing test. Second, the MG-Owner trap is partly memory-mediated: LEGACY keeps MG-Owner Y13 insurance at `0.390`, while CLEAN Full and CLEAN Flat average `0.290` and `0.257`. Third, past-reference frequency does not materially depend on institutional policy inside CLEAN. Owner rates average `0.018` in both CLEAN conditions, and renter rates average `0.068` in CLEAN Full and `0.066` in CLEAN Flat, far below LEGACY means of `0.202` and `0.292`. Fourth, Flat slightly intensifies the CLEAN action-space collapse for renters by nudging relocation from `0.000` to `0.001`, though both remain far below LEGACY at `0.010`. Fifth, removing endogenous institutions moves `insurance_rate_sfha` from `0.829` toward `0.787`, closer to the empirical upper bound without restoring calibration. Sixth, the Full-versus-Flat MG-Owner do-nothing delta is `0.027`, against seed SDs of `0.005` for CLEAN Full and `0.015` for CLEAN Flat, so uncertainty needs to be shown rather than hidden.

## 8. Anomalies and caveats

Several caveats belong in the main results discussion. The first is the small but real trace-count anomaly: `CLN_Flat_123` contains `2,599` owner traces rather than `2,600`, which is why the panel totals `31,199` household decisions. The second is structural asymmetry. Full arms have separate institutional traces, but Flat arms do not instantiate government and insurance agents; instead, they expose households to fixed policy context. The Full-versus-Flat comparison is therefore a contrast between endogenous institutional adaptation and static policy baselines, not between two equally expressive institutional agents. The third caveat is missing-cell design. Without LEGACY Flat, Paper 3 cannot run a full factorial moderation test for institutional effects under permissive memory. A fourth caveat is uncertainty. The MG-Owner Y13 insurance ANOVA is not significant at conventional thresholds (`F=1.677`, `p=0.138`), and Tukey HSD rejects no pairwise comparison, so the strongest claims should stay at the pattern and mechanism level. Finally, EPI must remain a heuristic calibration summary rather than a hard threshold. It is useful because it compresses many benchmark directions, but the underlying benchmark rows are more informative than the scalar itself.

## 9. Paper 3 structure recommendations

Section 4 should open with the cross-paradigm dual-timeseries figure because it gives readers the behavioral map before asking them to parse mechanism. The second visual should be the CLEAN Full-versus-CLEAN Flat forest plot, which isolates the institutional contrast with pooled means and standard errors. The third should be the construct-action heatmap, because it carries the RQ3 mechanism argument while making the survey-PA override explicit. The ratchet timeseries should then anchor the dual-role memory section, and the 4-cell bubble should support the equity framing around MG-Owner and renter outcomes. The text should headline only a small set of hard numbers: the Year-13 LEGACY-versus-CLEAN past-reference gap, the CLEAN Full-versus-CLEAN Flat `insurance_rate_sfha` shift from `0.829` to `0.787`, and the MG-Owner Y13 insurance contrast of `0.390` versus `0.290` versus `0.257`. Everything else should support those claims rather than compete with them. This structure keeps the paper focused on behavior, institutional tuning, and memory mechanism instead of turning the results section into a benchmark inventory. The synthesis directory now cleanly supports that selective citation strategy.

## 10. Open questions for Yang review

Three review questions are now precise enough to merit direct attention. First, is the missing LEGACY Flat arm important enough to justify a follow-on run, or is the current 6-arm panel sufficient if the limitation is stated plainly? Second, how should the paper balance the two roles of permissive memory: as a source of undesirable rationalization ratchet and as a source of realistic continuity and action diversity? Third, should Section 4 foreground calibration movement or subordinate it to the construct-action mechanism, given that Flat policy improves the SFHA insurance benchmark directionally but does not restore it to range? A fourth question concerns the Traditional FLOODABM comparison. Because the figure juxtaposes single-run Traditional output with single-seed LEGACY and pooled CLEAN conditions, should FLOODABM be framed as a reference trajectory rather than as a statistical comparator? Finally, Yang may want to decide whether the survey-PA override belongs in the main results text or in methods. The mechanism argument is cleaner when that override is stated explicitly, but the paper may prefer to keep the results section visually simpler. The current synthesis supports either choice.
