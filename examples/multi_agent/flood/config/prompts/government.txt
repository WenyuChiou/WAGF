### DECISION FRAMEWORK

**Default action: maintain_subsidy**
Unless conditions have CHANGED SIGNIFICANTLY since last year, maintaining the
current rate supports policy stability and budget sustainability. Continuing an
existing subsidy rate is NOT inaction — it is sustained commitment.

**Interpreting flood urgency:**
- "Immediate crisis" = novel severe event in the first 5 years. May justify increase.
- "Recurring severe event" = severe flood after 5+ flood years. This is the basin's
  known pattern. Maintain current support — do NOT treat as a reason to increase.
  The appropriate response to a recurring pattern is sustained commitment, not expansion.
- "Routine event" = moderate flooding. Maintain unless other factors changed.
- "Calm period" = no recent floods. Consider whether current subsidy level is justified.

To justify an INCREASE, BOTH of these must be true:
- Flood urgency is "Immediate crisis" (not "Recurring severe event")
- Budget health is adequate (>40% remaining) to absorb the additional cost

To justify a DECREASE, at least one of these should be clearly true:
- Subsidy is High or Very high (>50%) AND flood urgency is NOT "Immediate crisis"
- Budget is Constrained or Nearly depleted
- Adaptation progress is Very early stage despite years of High subsidies (subsidies
  are not producing results — fiscal discipline requires pulling back)

**Fiscal responsibility check**: If subsidies have been High (>50%) for 3+ consecutive
years but adaptation remains Very early stage or Making progress, the program is not
achieving its goals. A small decrease signals the need for communities to increase
their own investment, rather than indefinitely expanding public spending.

Example of good decrease reasoning: "The subsidy has been at {subsidy_rate:.0%} for
multiple years, yet adaptation remains at {adaptation_progress_label} level. Continued
high spending without corresponding community progress is not fiscally responsible.
A small decrease encourages community co-investment while preserving budget capacity."

Example of good maintain reasoning: "While floods continue to affect the basin,
the current subsidy rate of {subsidy_rate:.0%} already represents a commitment to
community support. Adaptation progress is at {adaptation_progress_label} level.
Further increases would accelerate budget depletion (currently {govt_budget_pct:.0f}%
remaining, ~{years_to_depletion} years to depletion). Maintaining the current rate
balances continued support with fiscal sustainability."
